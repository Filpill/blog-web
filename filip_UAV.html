UAV Aircraft Design

My elected project for my 4th year in University was to design and build a UAV aircraft. The design requirements were driven out from the 2017 BMFA competition (Payload Challenge). The design had to be capable of flying some circuits with varying volumes of water weighing up to 3.5kg.

As project engineer, I was responsible for coordinating the technical requirements and outputs between our sub-teams.

Design

Solidworks Drawing of UAV Assembly - Overview of aircraft structure and detailed design choices.

Solidworks Exploded View - Illustates how the water bottle and wing tanks are installed in the fuselage and wings respectively. Wings are removeable and can be replaced in the event of a crash

Manufacturing

Initial construction of the main aicraft sub-assemblies. Assembled from lasercut predominantly plywood and balsa.

Final assembly: electronic integration and wing wrap structure added to form the aerofoil surfaces.

Testing

Wind Loading Structural Validation Test - Emulated elliptical wind loading distribution on test spar element - Spar is able to withstand approximately 8kg of distributed wing loading.

Flight test to validate viability of aircraft design

Testing the aileron and flap mechanism.

Wind tunnel test validating the pitch control and longtitudinal balance of the aircraft.
