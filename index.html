What Does Your Learning Process Look Like?

The process is typically reverse engineered from the end product to define the systems and work steps involved. The work steps and systems are further sub-divided to flesh out the problem that is being solved. The clarity of the project improves with better definition of the system requirements.

Partial attempts to implement ideas are made early on for the intended project. The reason this is done is because it immediately flags your deficiencies and/or lack of understanding. For me, this is the perfect compass for directing your learning requirements.

The roadblocks encountered along the way are the clearest signal of what you need to do next! It is simply a matter of knowing the right sequence of questions to ask.
